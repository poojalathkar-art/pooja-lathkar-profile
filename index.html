I've had a slightly unconventional career.

I started in education, moved into fundraising, then product, customer success, and eventually People & Culture. On paper, it looks like I couldn't make up my mind. But to me, it's always been the same problem viewed from different angles.

I've always been fascinated by why some organisations seem to bring out the best in people, while others, with equally smart people, struggle.

The more I worked across functions, the more I kept seeing the same thing — it's rarely about the people. The people were usually fine. It was everything around them — how the place was run, how managers showed up, what got rewarded.

I moved into People and Culture because it felt like the closest I could get to actually fixing the things I kept seeing.

AI makes me optimistic about that. For the first time, there are tools that can actually help us fix the systems around people.

An Edge I Bring

I got curious about what AI could actually do in People work. So I started building. The more I built, the clearer one thing became: as an organisation scales, the People team doesn't have to. AI can handle most of the operational weight. What it can't replace is the judgment that comes from knowing your people, reading what's really going on, and being present for the moments that matter.
